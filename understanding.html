What is Data Privacy?

Data privacy refers to the right of individuals to control how their personal information is collected, used, and shared. It’s a critical component of maintaining trust in today’s digital ecosystem.

Example:

When signing up for a social media account, users often provide personal details like their name, email, and date of birth. Companies may share this information with advertisers, raising concerns about data privacy.

Key Concepts of Data Privacy:

  • Data Ownership: Users own their personal data and decide how it’s used.
  • Transparency: Companies should clearly communicate their data collection and usage practices.
  • Consent: Users must give informed consent before their data is collected.
  • Accountability: Organizations must be held responsible for safeguarding user data.

Why is Data Privacy Important?

Protecting personal data prevents misuse of information, identity theft, and loss of trust. Data privacy ensures that users can safely interact online without risking their sensitive information.
